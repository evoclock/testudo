---
title: "Naming: Testudo"
---

Three layers of meaning, all relevant to what the project does.

## Layer 1: the tortoise

Testudo is the genus name for tortoises. Tortoises carry their shell with them wherever they go. The metaphor for an isolated agent runtime is direct: the container travels with the agent, the agent never executes outside it.

## Layer 2: the Roman formation

The Roman testudo was a legionary formation in which soldiers interlocked their shields above and around the unit, forming a continuous protective barrier against arrows and projectiles. Inside the formation, individual soldiers (agents) operated; the formation as a whole was hardened from outside attack. Multi-agent execution under coordinated isolation is the same shape.

## Layer 3: slow and steady

Tortoises are not fast. The runtime's posture is the same: deterministic, careful, rollback-friendly, security-conscious. Speed is a property of the agents that run inside Testudo, not of the runtime itself.
